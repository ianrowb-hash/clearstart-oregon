What you need
You need a properly rolled set of fingerprints on the FBI standard blue applicant fingerprint card (FD-258). This is required under ORS 137.225 before you can file your motion.
A
Find a fingerprinting location
Your local sheriff's office or police department can fingerprint you. Many UPS stores, notary offices, and private Livescan providers also offer this service. Search "fingerprinting services near me" or call your county sheriff.
B
Request the FD-258 blue card format
Tell them it's for an Oregon expungement (set aside) under ORS 137.225. Ask specifically for the FBI standard FD-258 fingerprint card format. Some providers can print the card for you on-site.
Typically $10–$25
C
Bring valid photo ID
Government-issued photo ID is required (driver's license, passport, or state ID). Also bring your Social Security Number.
D
Keep the physical card — do not mail it yet
You will mail the original fingerprint card together with your OSP form and $80 fee in the next step. Do not send it separately.

Upcoming
Why this comes before filing
Oregon law requires the OSP to run a nationwide background check through the FBI before your motion is reviewed. The results go directly to the prosecuting attorney. You send the OSP package at the same time as — or just before — you file with the court.
A
Download the OSP Request for Set Aside form
Get the official form from the Oregon State Police website or your county's DA office. Search "Oregon State Police Request for Set Aside Criminal Record Check form" or go to oregon.gov/osp.
B
Fill out the OSP form completely
Fill in your full name, DOB, case number, county, and charge(s). Write "MOTION FOR SETTING ASIDE CONVICTION" in the purpose field.
C
Get a certified check or money order for $80
Made payable to Oregon State Police. Personal checks and cash are not accepted. Available at your bank or post office.
$80 — certified check or money order only
D
Mail the OSP package
Mail all three together in one envelope: (1) completed OSP form, (2) original FD-258 fingerprint card, (3) $80 payment. Send to: Oregon State Police, Criminal Justice Information Services, PO Box 4395, Portland, OR 97208. Use certified mail so you have tracking.
~2–3 weeks for OSP to process

You need (1) a Motion to Set Aside and (2) a Declaration/Affidavit sworn under penalty of perjury. Both must be filed together. The affidavit must be signed in front of a notary or court clerk.

Do not skip this step
Your Declaration/Affidavit is a sworn statement under penalty of perjury. Oregon law requires it to be signed in front of a notary public or a circuit court deputy clerk. Unnotarized affidavits will be rejected.
A
Print your affidavit (do not sign yet)
Print the Declaration document we generated. Do not sign it until you are in front of the notary — signatures before the notary witness are invalid.
B
Find a notary
Your circuit court clerk's office can notarize it for free. Banks, UPS stores, libraries, and many insurance offices also offer notary services. Search "notary near me."
Free at court clerk · $5–$15 elsewhere

The notary must verify your identity. Bring your driver's license, passport, or state ID.
D

Sign and date the affidavit while the notary watches. The notary will then add their seal and signature.

5
File with the court & serve the DA
No court filing fee · Make 2 copies before filing
Upcoming
Good news — no court filing fee
Oregon Senate Bill 397 (effective January 1, 2022) eliminated the circuit court filing fee for expungements. You will not pay anything to the court. The only fee is the $80 OSP background check you already mailed.
A
Make 3 copies of your complete packet
Your packet includes: Motion to Set Aside, notarized Declaration/Affidavit, and any supporting documents. Make 3 total copies: 1 original to file, 1 copy to serve the DA, 1 copy for your records.
B
File the original at the circuit court clerk
Bring the original packet to the circuit court in the county where you were convicted. Tell the clerk you are filing a Motion to Set Aside under ORS 137.225. No fee required. They will stamp your copies and give you a hearing date or filing confirmation.
C
Serve the District Attorney's office
Deliver one copy of the full packet to the DA's office in the same county. Include: Motion, notarized Affidavit, a copy of the fingerprint card, and a copy of your $80 OSP payment receipt. Service can be in person or by certified mail.
D
Optional: Include police report
Including a copy of your police report from the original arrest can speed up the DA's review. Request it from the arresting agency (usually free or low cost).
Yes — filed and served
6
DA review period — wait up to 120 days
District Attorney has 120 days to object · Most cases go uncontested
Upcoming
What happens during this period
The Oregon State Police sends the background check results to the DA. The DA then has up to 120 days to review your motion and either (1) not object — in which case the judge will likely grant your motion without a hearing, or (2) file an objection — in which case the court will schedule a hearing.
A
No action needed from you right now
You do not need to contact the DA or court during this period. Simply wait for correspondence. If you gave the court your email, you may receive updates electronically.
Up to 120 days
B
If the DA does NOT object
The judge will review your motion and typically sign the Order to Set Aside without requiring you to appear in court. You'll receive a certified copy of the signed order by mail.
C
If the DA objects
The court will schedule a hearing. You'll be notified by mail of the date, time, and location. Bring your ID, a copy of your petition, and any supporting documents. Most hearings are brief. You may want to consult an attorney if there is an objection.
D
Crime victims may also be notified
Under Oregon law, crime victims have the right to be notified of the expungement motion and may object or request a hearing. This is rare but can extend the timeline.

Hearing scheduled — here's what to do
Attend the hearing at the date and time shown on your notice. Bring your photo ID, a copy of your petition, and any character letters or supporting documents. Be punctual and dress professionally. Most contested hearings are brief. If you're concerned, consider a consultation with an Oregon attorney — the Oregon State Bar Lawyer Referral Service can connect you at oregonstatebar.org.
7
Record sealed — you're done
LEDS updated · FBI record cleared · You can legally say it never happened
Final step
What "set aside" means for you
Once the judge signs the Order, your conviction or arrest is legally deemed to have never occurred under Oregon law (ORS 137.255). You can legally answer "no" on job applications, rental applications, and most other forms that ask about prior convictions.
A
Keep your certified copy of the Order
The court will send you a certified copy of the signed Order to Set Aside. Store it safely — this is your legal proof of expungement.
B
LEDS is automatically notified
The circuit court clerk sends a certified copy to the Oregon Law Enforcement Data System (LEDS). Oregon State Police then updates their database. This takes approximately 30–90 days to fully propagate.
30–90 days
C
FBI record updated
The Oregon State Police notifies the FBI to update or remove your record from the national criminal database. Allow up to 90 days for full propagation across all databases.
D
Private background check databases
Some private background check companies (e.g. Checkr, Sterling) maintain their own databases and may not update immediately. If a private check returns old information, you can dispute it directly with the company and provide your certified Order as proof.
E
Federal benefits and firearms
Oregon expungement restores most civil rights including, per a 2025 Oregon Court of Appeals ruling (Mohiadeen v. Washington County), the right to possess firearms. Federal benefits eligibility is governed by federal law and may differ — consult an attorney for federal employment or security clearances.
Your record is sealed. Congratulations.
